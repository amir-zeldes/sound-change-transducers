Reduplication

Reduplication is the repetition of part or all of a word form to produce a new word form, like 'bye bye' (which is not simply saying goodbye two separate times). In old Indo-European languages reduplication of part of a stem played an important role in forming different tenses:

  1. Present stems often repeated the first consonant followed by the vowel -i-:
    Greek di-dō-mi < * di-deh3-mi 'I give' from a stem *deh3
